Key Research Finding

Bootstrap Identifiability Analysis

A parametric bootstrap study (n = 1000 resamplings) on the dual-damage UMAT revealed a structural non-identifiability in the hydrolytic damage pathway when calibrated from short-term mechanical data alone.

A model achieving excellent short-term fit (<1.2% RMSE over 90 days) still carries a ≈22 percentage-point one-year prediction uncertainty — reducible only by experimental data beyond the bound-moisture trapping timescale (τtrap ≈ 166 days).

! UMAT dual-damage kernel (FORTRAN) SUBROUTINE UMAT(STRESS, STATEV, ...) ! Carter-Kibler transport Cm = CK_mobile(Ctot, kA, kB) Cb = Ctot - Cm ! Dual damage update DP = plasticisation(Cm, DP_prev) DH = hydrolysis(Cb, DH_prev) ! Effective stiffness Ceff = C0 * (1-DP) * (1-DH) END SUBROUTINE

🏒 hockey-blade-FEA

FE analysis of a CFRP ice hockey blade under slap shot, pass impact, and cyclic fatigue loading in Abaqus/Standard. Hashin failure criteria capture ply-level fibre and matrix damage; BK cohesive law governs delamination evolution. Fatigue extrapolation estimates full compressive fibre failure at ~400 cycles.
